The Elixir Stabilization Checklist

Everything I look at when I parachute into an Elixir codebase to make it calm again. Skim it. Skip what you've already got. The gaps are your roadmap.

1 Version control & change safety

  • Work happens on branches, merged via PRs Not committing straight to main. Every change is reviewable and revertable.
  • Any change can be reverted in minutes Small, atomic commits. You can back out a bad deploy without archaeology.
  • Main is always deployable Green build = shippable. No "don't deploy, Dave's mid-refactor."

2 Static analysis & consistency

  • Credo runs in CI, not just on someone's laptop Consistency enforced automatically, so review is about logic, not style.
  • Dialyzer / type checking is set up and green Catches whole classes of bugs before they reach production.
  • Formatter is enforced (mix format --check-formatted) Zero-effort consistency; no more diff noise from whitespace.

3 Tests you can trust

  • The suite is green and nobody ignores failures A suite people route around is worse than no suite — it launders false confidence.
  • No flaky tests (or they're quarantined and tracked) Flakiness trains your team to hit "re-run" and stop reading failures.
  • Coverage where it counts — money paths, auth, data integrity Not a coverage number; the *right* things covered.
  • Tests run fast enough that people actually run them A slow suite is a skipped suite.

4 CI & delivery

  • CI checks format, Credo, Dialyzer, tests — every PR The gate is automatic, not a reviewer remembering to look.
  • Builds are fast (cached deps, cached PLTs, parallelism) Slow CI is a tax on every single change your team ships.
  • Deploys are boring, repeatable, and reversible Shipping shouldn't require holding your breath.

5 Dependencies & the runtime

  • Elixir / Phoenix / LiveView are current (or on a plan to be) Falling behind compounds; the longer you wait, the scarier the jump.
  • No unmaintained or vulnerable deps (mix hex.audit, mix deps.unlock --check-unused) Know what you're standing on before it gives way.
  • You understand your OTP/supervision tree When something crashes at 2am, the process design is either your friend or your enemy.

6 Observability & performance

  • Errors are captured and someone actually triages them (Sentry, etc.) A Sentry nobody reads is a smoke alarm with the battery out.
  • You can see slow requests / slow queries You can't fix "the app feels slow" — you fix the N+1 on the dashboard.
  • The database has the indexes its queries need The single most common source of "it got slow as we grew."
